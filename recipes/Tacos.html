Mexican birria tacos repice

Mexican birria tacos, Jalisco-style, made with braised beef that's slow-cooked in a fragrant 3-chile sauce with a delicious spice mix. Crispy tacos, and tender, mouth-watering beef team up with melted Mexican cheese in this impressive meal. This recipe takes a little time but it's so worth it!

Ingredients

  • Succulent beef (typically brisket or chuck)
  • Aromatic spices (such as cloves, bay leaves, and cumin)
  • Dried chilies (like guajillo, ancho, or pasilla)
  • Garlic cloves
  • Onions
  • Vinegar
  • Oregano
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper
  • Warm corn tortillas
  • Optional toppings: chopped onions, cilantro, lime wedges, salsa

Steps

  1. Prepare the dried chilies by removing stems and seeds, then toast them lightly in a dry skillet.
  2. Boil the chilies in water until softened, then blend with garlic, onions, vinegar, oregano, cinnamon, bay leaves, salt, and pepper to make a smooth marinade.
  3. Coat the beef with the marinade and refrigerate overnight for flavors to meld.
  4. Slow-cook the marinated beef in a Dutch oven or slow cooker until tender and easily shredded.
  5. Remove the beef, shred it with forks, and strain the cooking liquid for dipping.
